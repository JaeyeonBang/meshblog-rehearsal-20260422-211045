# meshblog — Design Context

> Source of truth for all design work on this project. Read this before any /impeccable, /layout, /typeset, /colorize, /polish, or related skill run.

## Users

**Primary**: Jaeyeon Bang — a bilingual (Korean + English) engineer keeping a long-running second brain. They write in both languages, cross-link ideas between them, and want the site to feel like a *reading room*, not a dashboard.

**Secondary**: readers who arrive from links, search, or an RSS feed. They read one post at a time, follow a related link, and occasionally open the graph to see where a concept sits in the larger mesh.

**Context of use**:
- Quiet evening reading at a desk or in bed, often on phone
- Occasional navigation via /graph to trace a concept's neighbours
- Rare, deliberate visits — not a scroll-feed, not a social product

**The job they're trying to get done**:
- Read a single piece of writing comfortably end-to-end
- Follow the thread — related notes, concept clusters, Q&A chips — without feeling like they're being funnelled
- Trust that what they're reading was written by a human who cared

## Brand Personality

**Three words**: *quiet · editorial · considered*

Not: modern, clean, minimal (those are dead categories). Not: maximalist, playful, neon-on-dark (wrong register). Not: "dev blog" — no monospace-as-personality, no terminal aesthetics, no neon cyan.

**Voice**: a long-form essay in a small print magazine. Confident enough to use one serif typeface and hold the page with it. Careful enough to set its own margins.

**Emotional goal**: when a reader lands here, they should instinctively *slow down* by half a beat. The site should read as "someone's personal journal that happened to be made public," not "personal brand."

## Aesthetic Direction

**Direction**: editorial · paper · ink. A considered print-magazine sensibility translated to the browser. Think: the interior pages of a small literary quarterly, not the cover.

**Theme**: **light** by default. This is a reading surface used at a desk, on a phone at night under a lamp. Dark mode stays available via `prefers-color-scheme` but is a warm off-black with reduced chroma — not a glowing-accent-on-pure-black dashboard. The brief is "lamp-lit library at night," not "trading desk."

**Palette**: monochrome + ink. Near-neutrals tinted ~3–5° warm (toward paper/buff) so surfaces feel like aged uncoated paper rather than clinical white. Accent is a single quiet ink — a deep, slightly desaturated navy/indigo, used *sparingly* for links and focus only. No blue-to-purple gradients. No neon. No cyan.

**Typography**:
- **Body / prose**: **Literata** — a humanist serif designed for long-form reading on screens. Variable-weight, generous x-height, opens up Korean-Latin mixed lines without the awkward height mismatch most serifs produce.
- **UI / headings / Korean runs**: **Pretendard** — already bundled, already subsetted, covers the Korean glyph range beautifully and pairs as a quieter sans next to Literata.
- No display face. No monospace in UI chrome. Code in `<pre>`/`<code>` only.

**References (aesthetic)**:
- Robin Rendle's blog (single serif, generous margins, one accent)
- *The Paris Review* web issues (narrow column, typographic restraint)
- Craig Mod's writing sites (paper tint, editorial headers)
- Notation and marginalia traditions — underlines over box-strokes

**Anti-references (what this must NOT look like)**:
- Dev blog "hacker" vibe (Space Mono, terminal green, ASCII art)
- Corporate documentation portal (Inter, cards, sidebar nav)
- Obsidian default theme ported to web (purple graph, grey boxes)
- AI-app aesthetic (glass cards, gradient text, cyan glow, rounded-icon-over-heading)

## Design Principles

1. **Paper before pixels.** Every surface starts from a warm near-white with tinted neutrals; pure `#fff` and pure `#000` never appear. OKLCH only — hex literals live only in `tokens.css` as fallback commentary.
2. **One accent, rarely.** A single ink-navy reserved for active links, focus rings, and the rare interactive affordance. If everything is the accent colour, nothing is. The graph is monochrome; posts are monochrome; only hover + focus earn the accent.
3. **Serif holds the page.** Long reading is set in Literata. Don't reach for a display face for impact — use size, weight variation, and vertical rhythm. Never gradient-fill text. Never track-out all-caps headings longer than two words.
4. **Rules, not boxes.** Separate content with hair rules (1px), whitespace, or a single top-border — never with full-bordered cards stacked on each other. Especially: no left-stripe accent borders. Ever.
5. **Asymmetry over centering.** The homepage and article layouts lean left or sit in a narrow column offset from centre. Centered layouts are reserved for moments that genuinely want ceremony (404, perhaps the footer mark).
6. **Mobile reads like print.** Narrow viewport is the *first* layout we design, not the last. A phone in hand at night should feel like a pocket-size printed zine — generous line-height, comfortable column, no horizontal chrome.
